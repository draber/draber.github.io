What are the different parts good for?

  • Score so far

    How far have you been moving ahead already and long is the way to the top? And - and that's a tiny spoiler - how many points will you need to have to become Queen Bee? That's the sort of question you get answered here.

  • Spoilers

    So you found a good bunch of words, discovered the Pangram but now you wonder if there are words with four letters left that you've been missing. Maybe there is another Pangram hidden in the hive? Or at least a good long term that moves you to the next level? The Spoiler panel won't tell you the words, but it will tell you, how many of each size lay still ahead.

  • Spill the beans

    What was the name of this bird again, that you only know from solving crosswords? You know what it starts with, nut what came after the 3rd letter agin?   • Steps to success

    You've certainly seen this one before, which is no wonder, because it's pretty close to the Rankings pop-up that is already part of Spelling Bee. But this one you can keep open while you're playing and - as an added bonus - it reveals how far the road to Queen Bee is. The tier that you're currently in is highlighted.

  • Surrender

    You aren't ready to declare defeat already, are you?

    Well, if you are, no need to wait until 3:00 a.m. ET anymore - with this button you can resolve the game with a mouse clicks. But don't expect to be crowned Queen Bee! All the words you didn't find will be linked to the respective terms in Google. Maybe this will help you next time!

    So you like only some of the toys here, but not all of them? Get rid of those you don't like, they won't bother you anymore. Unless of course you use the assistant on another browser, then you'll have to do it all over again.

What is a bookmarklet?

Bookmarklets are small pieces of code that can enhance the functionality of a website. They reside, just like regular bookmarks, in your browser‚Äôs bookmark menu.


Does it work everywhere?

The assistant is not suitable for the mobile version of the game and has only been tested in the desktop version. It requires a modern browser and won‚Äôt work in Internet Explorer.

Does it not spoil the game?

Other people have been creating tools with a similar purpose which - according to this article - the Spelling Bee community seems to like well.

Use the Assistant sparsely, don't launch it before having solved a good chunk of the game on your own. Don't open the Spoilers panel before you really need to. Only hit the magic button when you eventually run completely out of ideas. At least for me it works well this way.
